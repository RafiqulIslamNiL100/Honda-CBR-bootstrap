Frequently Asked Questions

Pump your tyres to the right pressure Possibly the simplest aspect of bike maintenance is having your tyres pumped to the right pressure. What is the right pressure? That’s simple, too - it’s written on the sidewall of your tyres, just take a look. You’ll notice the tyre manufacture has a recommended range rather than one absolute pressure.

When you buy a new helmet it fits very snug. If you turn or shake your head it remains stuck to your noggin like glue. To determine if your helmet has loosened try this test. Shake your head side-to-side. Does it remain stuck to your head or does it slide around? If your helmet slides around while shaking your head, this means your padding has become compacted and may be time for a replacement.

Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely. It is common for cables to stretch and for air to build up in hydraulic brakes.
